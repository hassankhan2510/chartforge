You are analyzing 3 chart screenshots for a SCALP trade setup. The charts are provided in this exact order:
1. **1-Minute Chart** — Micro price action, tick-level patterns, immediate order flow
2. **5-Minute Chart** — Short-term momentum, micro-trend structure, key micro-levels
3. **15-Minute Chart** — Scalp bias context, higher structure within the micro-timeframe

=== YOUR ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK ===

For EACH chart, you must identify and describe ALL of the following in explicit detail:

**A. CANDLESTICK PATTERNS (Micro-Level)**
- Look for: Pin bars, engulfing candles, doji, spinning tops, hammer/shooting star
- Note: Candle body-to-wick ratio (long wicks = rejection, small bodies = indecision)
- Identify: Any 2-3 candle reversal patterns (morning/evening star, three soldiers/crows)
- Context: Where these patterns appear relative to key levels (at support/resistance = high value)

**B. PRICE ACTION STRUCTURE**
- Identify the MICRO-TREND: Is price making higher highs/higher lows (bullish) or lower highs/lower lows (bearish)?
- Find: Range-bound zones vs trending segments
- Mark: Break of structure (BOS) points — where price broke a previous swing high/low
- Identify: Change of character (CHoCH) — the first sign of potential reversal
- Note: Any liquidity sweeps (price spikes beyond a level then quickly reverses)

**C. KEY MICRO-LEVELS**
- Identify: Immediate support and resistance levels visible on the chart
- Note: Price zones where there were multiple touches (tested 2+ times = stronger level)
- Find: Round number levels that price is reacting to
- Identify: Gaps (fair value gaps) — areas where price moved quickly leaving unfilled zones

**D. MOMENTUM & DIVERGENCE**
- If any oscillators/indicators are visible on the chart, analyze them
- Look for: RSI divergence (price making new high but RSI making lower high = bearish divergence)
- Note: Momentum acceleration vs deceleration (are the candles getting bigger or smaller?)
- Identify: Volume divergence if volume bars are visible

**E. ORDER FLOW CLUES**
- Large candles with high volume suggest institutional activity
- Clusters of small candles at a level suggest absorption (someone is accumulating/distributing)
- Quick rejections (long wicks) at a level suggest aggressive orders sitting there
- Breakouts on increasing candle size = genuine; decreasing size = likely false breakout

**F. MULTI-TIMEFRAME ALIGNMENT FOR SCALP**
- Does the 1m trend align with the 5m trend? If yes, scalp WITH the 5m direction
- Does the 15m chart show a clear bias? Scalp only in the 15m direction if possible
- Look for: 5m support/resistance levels that align with 1m entry triggers
- Optimal scalp: 15m gives direction, 5m gives structure, 1m gives entry timing
