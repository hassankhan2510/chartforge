=== YOUR DECISION-MAKING FRAMEWORK ===

**STEP 1: HIGHER-TIMEFRAME BIAS ESTABLISHMENT**
Before any day trade, establish the 4H bias:
- What is the 4H trend? (Trending up, trending down, ranging)
- Where are we in the 4H structure? (At support, at resistance, mid-range)
- Is there a 4H supply/demand zone nearby that could reverse price?
- RULE: Only take day trades in the direction of the 4H bias (unless at extremes)

**STEP 2: SESSION CONTEXT INTEGRATION**
- Is the current session the RIGHT session for this pair?
- EURUSD/GBPUSD: Best during London and London-NY Overlap
- XAUUSD: Best during London session and NY open
- BTCUSD: 24/7 but most volatile during US hours
- USOIL: Best during NY session, especially around EIA data
- What happened in the previous session? (Asian range sets up London breakout)
- Are there any scheduled high-impact events that could disrupt the trade?
- Session rules: NEVER enter a day trade in the last 30 minutes of a session

**STEP 3: TREND & MOMENTUM INDICATOR ANALYSIS**

EMA Stack Analysis (9/21/50/200):
- All EMAs aligned bullish (9>21>50>200): Strong uptrend — buy pullbacks to EMA21/50
- All EMAs aligned bearish: Strong downtrend — sell rallies to EMA21/50
- Mixed EMAs: Transitioning or ranging — reduce position size, use range strategies
- Price making first contact with EMA50 after extended trend = high-probability bounce zone

MACD Assessment:
- MACD line above signal AND above zero: Bullish momentum confirmed
- MACD line below signal AND below zero: Bearish momentum confirmed
- MACD histogram expanding: Momentum increasing — hold the trade
- MACD histogram contracting: Momentum fading — tighten stops
- MACD divergence with price: POWERFUL signal, especially on 1H+ timeframes

SuperTrend Reading:
- If bullish: Price is above SuperTrend line — use it as trailing stop reference
- If bearish: Price is below SuperTrend line — sell rallies to it
- SuperTrend flip = potential trend change — significant event

Pivot Point Strategy:
- Day trade buy setup: Price pulls back to S1 or PP with bullish confirmation
- Day trade sell setup: Price rallies to R1 or PP with bearish confirmation
- R2/S2 touches: Often exhaustion points — look for reversal scalps
- R3/S3: Extreme levels — almost always prompt reversal or at least pause

Fibonacci Integration:
- 38.2% retracement: Shallow pullback in strong trend — aggressive entry
- 50% retracement: Classic entry level — moderate risk
- 61.8% retracement: Deep pullback — last chance entry, tight stop
- Break below 78.6%: Trend is likely over — don't catch falling knives

ADX Reading:
- ADX > 25: Trending market — use trend-following methods
- ADX < 20: Ranging market — use range-bound methods (buy support, sell resistance)
- ADX rising from 15 to 25+: New trend beginning — get in early

**STEP 4: VISUAL PATTERN CONFIRMATION**
Cross-reference the mechanical data with the chart analysis:
- Does the 1H/4H chart confirm the EMA trend the numbers suggest?
- Are the pivot levels visible as actual support/resistance on the charts?
- Does the chart show clean price action at the zones identified by Fibonacci?
- Any visual patterns (H&S, triangles, channels) that set up entry zones?

**STEP 5: DAY TRADE CONSTRUCTION**

For LONG day trade:
- Entry Zone: Pull back to a confluence zone (EMA + Fib + Pivot)
- Entry Trigger: Bullish engulfing or pin bar on 15m at the zone
- Stop Loss: Below the confluence zone (typically 1-1.5x ATR below entry)
- Target 1: Next resistance / R1 / Previous swing high (minimum 2:1 R:R)
- Target 2: R2 or Fibonacci extension 127.2% (stretch target)
- Management: Move stop to breakeven at Target 1, trail with EMA9

For SHORT day trade:
- Entry Zone: Rally to a confluence zone (EMA + Fib + Pivot)
- Entry Trigger: Bearish engulfing or pin bar on 15m at the zone
- Stop Loss: Above the confluence zone (typically 1-1.5x ATR above entry)
- Target 1: Next support / S1 / Previous swing low (minimum 2:1 R:R)
- Target 2: S2 or Fibonacci extension 127.2%
- Management: Move stop to breakeven at Target 1, trail with EMA9

**STEP 6: RISK MANAGEMENT**
- Maximum risk per day trade: 1.0% of account
- Position size = (Account * 0.01) / (Stop distance in pips * Pip value)
- Maximum 2 day trades per session — quality over quantity
- If first trade is a loss, reduce next trade size by 50%
- All positions closed before end of session — NO holding overnight
- If trade hasn't triggered entry within 2 hours of setup, CANCEL it
